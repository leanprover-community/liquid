12 Condensed abelian groups

Remark 12.1
#

For the time being, the following facts will be used without proof in this text. (They have or will be formalized in Lean though.)

  • There is a natural functor \(\operatorname{Top}\to \operatorname{Cond}(\operatorname{Sets})\).

  • The category of condensed abelian groups (resp. condensed \(R\)-modules) is an abelian category with enough projectives. For \(S\) an extremally disconnected set, the objects \(\mathbb Z[S]\) (resp. \(R[S]\)) is projective.

Definition 12.2
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.9

Lean declarations

  • CompHausFiltPseuNormGrp₁.presheaf

There is a natural functor

\begin{align*} \text{CHPNG} & \to \operatorname{Cond}(\operatorname{Ab}) \\ M & \mapsto (S \mapsto M(S)) \end{align*}

where \(M(S)\) is defined to be collection of functions \(f \colon S \to M\) that factor as continuous through \(M_c\), for some \(c\). In symbols:

\[ M(S) = \{ f \colon S \to M \mid \exists c, f(S) \subset M_c \text{ and $f \colon S \to M_c$ is continuous}\} . \]

There is a natural functor

\begin{align*} \text{CHPNGTinv}_1 & \to \mathrm{Mod}^{\mathrm{cond}}_{\mathbb Z[T^{-1}]} \\ M & \mapsto (S \mapsto M(S)). \end{align*}

Indeed, there is clearly a functorial action of \(T^{-1}\) on \(M(S)\).

Lemma 12.4
#

Uses

  • Definition 12.2

Let \(S = \varprojlim S_i\) be a profinite set. Then \(\mathbb Z[S]\) is naturally a profinitely filtered pseudo-normed group, via \(\mathbb Z[S]_{\le c} = \varprojlim \mathbb Z[S_i]_{\le c}\), where \(\mathbb Z[S_i]_{\le c}\) is the set \(\{ \sum _{s \in S_i} n_s[s] \mid \sum _s |n_s| \le c\} \).

There is a natural isomorphism between the free condensed abelian group \(\mathbb Z[S]\) and the colimit \(\bigcup _c \mathbb Z[S]_{\le c}\) of condensed sets.

Proof ▼

For now, see Lemma 2.1 of [ Sch20 ] .

Lemma 12.9
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.3

Let \(0 {\lt} r {\lt} r' {\lt} 1\) be real numbers. Let \(S\) be a profinite set, and let \(V\) be an \(r\)-normed \(\mathbb Z[T^{\pm 1}]\)-module. Then \(\operatorname{Ext}_{\mathrm{Mod}^{\mathrm{cond}}_{\mathbb Z[T^{-1}]}}^i(\mathcal L_{r'}(S), V) = 0\) for all \(i {\gt} 0\).

Proof ▼

Consider the long exact sequence of Ext-groups arising form the short exact sequence (Lemma 12.8)

\[ 0 \to \mathbb Z[T^{-1}][S] \to \mathcal L_{r'}(S) \to \overline{\mathcal{M}_{r'}}(S) \to 0 \]

by applying \(\operatorname{Ext}^*(\_ , V)\).

By Lemma 12.6 all groups \(\operatorname{Ext}_{\operatorname{Cond}(\operatorname{Ab})}^i(\mathbf{Z}[S], V)\) vanish for \(i {\gt} 0\). And by Lemma 12.7 all groups \(\operatorname{Ext}_{\mathrm{Mod}^{\mathrm{cond}}_{\mathbb Z[T^{-1}]}}^i(\overline{\mathcal M_{r'}}(S), V)\) vanish for \(i \ge 0\). The result follows.

Theorem 12.11 Clausen–Scholze
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.2
  • Definition 10.1

Let \(0 {\lt} p' {\lt} p \le 1\) be real numbers, let \(S\) be a profinite set, and let \(V\) be a \(p\)-Banach space. Let \(\mathcal M_{p'}(S)\) be the space of \(p'\)-measures on \(S\). Then

\[ \operatorname{Ext}^i_{\operatorname{Cond}(\operatorname{Ab})}(\mathcal M_{p'}(S),V) = 0 \]

for \(i \ge 1\).

Proof ▼

By Lemma 12.9, it suffices to prove

\[ \operatorname{Ext}^i_{\operatorname{Cond}(\operatorname{Ab})}(\mathcal M_{p'}(S),V) \cong \operatorname{Ext}_{\mathrm{Mod}^{\mathrm{cond}}_{\mathbb Z[T^{-1}]}}^i(\mathcal L_{r'}(S), V). \]
