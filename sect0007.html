2.3 Laurent Measures

Definition 2.3.1 ✓
#

Lean declarations

  • laurent_measures

Let \(0 {\lt} r' {\lt} 1\) be a real number, and let \(S\) be a finite set. Then \(\mathcal L_{r'}(S)\) denotes the group

\[ \mathcal L_{r'}(S)=\{ \sum _{s\in S,n\in \mathbf{Z}}a_{n,s}T^n \vert a_{n,s}\in \mathbf{Z}, \sum _{n\in \mathbf{Z}, s\in S}\lvert a_{n,s}\rvert r^n {\lt} + \infty \} . \]
The group \(\mathcal L_{r'}(S)\) is filtered by the subsets

\[ \mathcal L_{r'}(S)_{\leq c}=\bigl \{ \sum _{s\in S,n\in \mathbf{Z}}a_{n,s}T^n \vert a_{n,s}\in \mathbf{Z}, \sum _{n\in \mathbf{Z}, s\in S}\lvert a_{n,s}\rvert r^n \leq c\bigr \} \]

for \(c{\gt} 0\). Each subset \(\mathcal L_{r'}(S)_{\leq c}\) can be written as

\[ \mathcal L_{r'}(S)_{\leq c}=\varprojlim _{A}\mathcal L_{r'}(S)_{A,\leq c} \]

where \(A\) runs through the finite subsets of \(\mathbf{Z}\) and \(\mathcal L_{r'}(S)_{A,\leq c}\) is the finite set

\[ \mathcal L_{r'}(S)_{A,\leq c}=\bigl \{ \sum _{s\in S,n\in A}a_{n,s}T^n \vert a_{n,s}\in \mathbf{Z}, \sum _{n\in A, s\in S}\lvert a_{n,s}\rvert r^n\leq c\bigr \} . \]

This defines a profinite topology on \(\mathcal L_{r'}(S)_{\leq c}\), for each \(c{\gt}0\).

Definition 2.3.2 ✓
#

Lean declarations

  • laurent_measures

Let \(0 {\lt} r' {\lt} 1\) be a real number, and let \(S\) be a profinite set with a presentation \(S=\varprojlim S_i\), where all \(S_i\) are finite. For all \(c{\gt}0\), let \(\mathcal L_{r'}(S)_{\leq c}\) denote the projective limit

\[ \mathcal L_{r'}(S)_{\leq c}=\varprojlim \mathcal L_{r'}(S_i)_{\leq c}, \]

endowed with the projective limit topology, and set

\[ \mathcal L_{r'}(S)=\varinjlim _{c {\gt} 0}\mathcal L_{r'}(S)_{\leq c} \]

Now fix \(0{\lt} \xi {\lt}1\) and let \(x\in \mathbf{R}_{\geq 0}\). For simplicity, denote by \(\mathbf{Z}(\! (T)\! )_{r'}\) the group \(\mathcal L_{r'}(\ast )\). The next theorem is the first statement of Theorem 6.9 in  [ Sch20 ] .

For all \(n\in \mathbf{N}\), set

\[ Y_\xi (x, n)= \begin{cases} x& \text{ if } n = 0 \\ Y_\xi (x,n-1) - \left\lfloor \frac{Y_\xi (x,n-1)}{\xi ^{n-1}} \right\rfloor \xi ^{n-1} & \text{ if } n \ge 1 \end{cases} \]

Lemma 2.3.5 ✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 2.3.3

Lean declarations

  • theta.ϑ_surjective

The map \(\vartheta _{\xi }\) is surjective.

Proof ▼

Pick \(x\in \mathbf{R}\), and consider the power series

\[ F(T)=\sum _{n\geq 0}\left\lfloor \frac{Y_\xi (x,n)}{\xi ^n}\right\rfloor T^n\in \mathbf{Z}[\! [T]\! ] \]

where \(\{ Y_\xi (x,n)\} _{n}\) is the sequence defined in Definition 2.3.4. The fact that \(0{\lt}\xi {\lt}1\) ensures that the series converges on the open unit disk, and it can be proven that \(F(\xi )=x\).

Given any finite set \(S\), the above map \(\vartheta _{\xi }\) defines a map \(\vartheta _{\xi ,S}\colon \mathcal L_{r'}(S)\longrightarrow \mathcal M_{p'}(S)\) by extending \(\vartheta _{\xi }\) componentwise. For the purpose of this project, the special case where \(\xi =1/2\) is enough, and we write \(\theta _S\) to denote \(\vartheta _{\xi ,S}\).

Theorem 2.3.7 ✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 2.3.3

Let \(0{\lt}p'{\lt}1\) be a real number, let \(S\) be a profinite set, and let \(r'\) denote \(\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{p'}\). The map

\[ \theta _{S}\colon \mathcal L_{r'}(S)\longrightarrow \mathcal M_{p'}(S) \]

induced by evaluating \(T\) at \(1/2\) is continuous and surjective.

Proof ▼

The proof of surjectivity for finite \(S\) follows immediately from Lemma 2.3.5, and a general argument shows that the surjectivity is preserved by taking inverse limits. Similarly, the proof of continuity is a direct consequence of Lemma 2.3.6.

With Theorem 2.3.7 at our disposal, we can move on and analyse the kernel of the map \(\theta _{S}\).
